Abstract:
This data set shows the national forest and ranger district boundaries in Montana, and national forest land that is outside the forest boundaries. It does not distinguish among federal, state, and private lands that are inside the forest boundaries.

In the Arc/Info coverage version of this data, National forests are represented by a 'region.forest' feature class and ranger districts by a 'region.district' feature class. In the shapefile version, each polygon has a field for the forest and district name and attributes of the districts in its attribute table, and there is an additional table called forests.dbf that contains information about the forests.

These data are extracted from the statewide land ownership and managed areas layer (stew) maintained by the MTNHP.

Note that these data do not conform entirely with official national forest boundaries: (1) the boundaries included here have been expanded to encompass national forest system lands that lie outside of the official forest boundary and (2) many small and discrete polygons that have no national forest ownership but were (and are) included in the officially designated forest boundary are not included in these data.

Use constraints:
These data show the approximate location of National Forests in Montana. They are not to be used to determine the legal owner of the land at any particular location, nor do they imply public access to any lands. Many private and state-owned parcels lie within the national forest boundaries depicted in these data.

Data Quality Information:
Attribute accuracy report:
Forest and District names and contact information (address and phone) are taken from various National Forest websites (accessed from: http://www.fs.fed.us/recreation/map/state_list.shtml.)

Completeness report:
Complete and current as of publication date. Changes do occur in both national forest and ranger district boundaries as well as with names and contact information.


Horizontal Positional Accuracy Report:
This is basically 1:100000 scale (100K) data with a horizontal accuracy of approximately 170 feet.

Process step:
Polygons with CODES containing 'fs' are copied from stew to this layer. The attributes are added and populated with the relevant forest and ranger district information. Then region.district and region.forest feature classes are created using 'regiondissolve'.
Process date: 09/25/2002
Process step:
The name of the Kings Hill Ranger District in the Lewis and Clark National Forest was changed to White Sulphur Springs.
Process date: 06/23/2008
